## Everpure's Unique KPIs (Your Cheat Sheet)

These are the non-standard metrics the street watches closely.
Knowing these will signal you speak the CFO's language.

### 1. Subscription ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)
- **FY26:** $1.92B (+16% YoY)
- **What it is:** Annualized value of all active subscription
  contracts + on-demand billings x4
- **Why it matters:** The single best measure of the hardware-to-
  subscription transition. Wall Street uses this to justify the
  valuation premium vs. Dell/NetApp.

### 2. RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations)
- **FY26:** $3.67B (+40% YoY)
- **What it is:** Contracted but not yet recognized revenue
  (deferred revenue $2.23B + unbilled $1.44B)
- **Why it matters:** 40% growth vs. 16% revenue growth = massive
  future revenue backlog building. This was the standout number
  in the earnings release.

### 3. TCV Sales (Total Contract Value)
- **What it is:** Bookings value of Evergreen//One and similar
  consumption/subscription deals in a given period
- **Recent trend:** Storage-as-a-Service TCV grew 25% YoY in Q3

### 4. Large Deal Growth ($5M+ deals)
- **Trend:** +80% YoY in Q4
- **Why it matters:** Shows enterprise/hyperscaler traction,
  not just SMB volume

### 5. Customer Count & Penetration
- **Total customers:** 14,500+ (added 1,100 in FY26)
- **Fortune 500 penetration:** 64%
- **Global 2000 penetration:** 42%

## Financial Reporting Nuances Worth Knowing

**Revenue recognition complexity:**
Everpure has a hybrid model - product sales (recognized on
delivery) + subscription services (recognized ratably over
contract term). The mix is shifting toward subscriptions, which
*compresses near-term revenue recognition* but builds a more
valuable, predictable stream. CFOs love talking about this
tension.

**Subscription gross margins are exceptional:**
Subscription services run at ~77% gross margin vs. product
margins that fluctuate with NAND pricing. As the mix shifts,
blended margins should expand. This is the HPE playbook
Robbiati already ran.

**Hyperscaler revenue is lumpy:**
Robbiati specifically flagged that hyperscaler revenue "is
not linear" - concentrated in Q3/Q4 of FY27. This creates
quarter-to-quarter volatility that can confuse the street.
The CFO will be managing this narrative carefully.

**Hyperscaler margins are accretive:**
Counter-intuitive: hyperscaler gross margins are 75-85%
because Everpure only provides the DirectFlash IP/modules,
while hyperscalers procure their own NAND. High-margin
licensing, not hardware resale.

**GAAP vs. Non-GAAP gap:**
Stock-based comp is significant (common in tech). Non-GAAP
operating margin was 21.3% in Q4. Always clarify which
basis someone is referencing.

## Strategic Context for Your Conversation

### The Rebrand (Feb 23, 2026)
Pure Storage is now **Everpure**. Ticker stays PSTG. Trading
under new name March 5. The name signals evolution from
"storage company" to "data platform company." Customer feedback
drove it - old name was limiting category perception.

### The Acquisition: 1touch
- Data intelligence & orchestration startup (founded 2017)
- Product called **Kontextual** - discovers, classifies, and
  contextualizes enterprise data across any environment
- 500% YoY bookings growth, zero churn
- Strategic fit: makes data "AI-ready at the source"
- Expected to close Q2 FY27, terms not disclosed

### The Tech Moat: DirectFlash
- Proprietary flash modules (150TB now, 300TB coming)
- Manages flash at the media level, bypassing standard SSD
  controllers. Competitors (Dell, HPE) use off-the-shelf SSDs.
- 5x density advantage = less power, cooling, space
- This is what won the hyperscaler business

### AI Positioning
- FlashBlade//EXA for AI-scale workloads
- Industry-leading MLPerf and SPEC Storage AI benchmarks
- First EXA customer secured (GPU cloud provider who
  switched from a competitor after performance tests)
- Dozens more in advanced discussions

## Risks to Be Aware Of

- **Component cost inflation:** NAND, memory, CPUs, GPUs all
  rising due to AI infrastructure build-outs outpacing supply
- **"Unpredictable component shortages"** (CEO's words) could
  cause shipment delays
- **Hyperscaler concentration risk:** Large deals = lumpy
  revenue and customer concentration
- **Tariff exposure:** Evergreen//One subscription model is
  positioned as a hedge against tariff-driven price spikes
